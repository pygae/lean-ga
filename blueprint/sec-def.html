1.2 Clifford algebras - definition

Definition 1.2.1 Clifford algebra ✓
#

Lean declarations

  • clifford_algebra

Let \(M\) be a module over a commutative ring \(R\), equipped with a quadratic form \(Q: V \to R\).

A Clifford algebra over \(M\), denoted \(\mathcal{G}(M)\), is the quotient of the tensor algebra \(T(M)\) by the equivalence relation \(v \otimes v \sim Q(v)\).

Remark 1.2.2
#

In literatures, the quotient is by the two-sided ideal \(I_Q\) generated from the set \(\{ v \otimes v - Q(v) \: \vert \: v \in V \} \).

As of writing, mathlib does not have direct support for two-sided ideals, but it does support the equivalent operation of taking the quotient by a suitable closure of a relation like \(v \otimes v \approx Q(v)\).

Hence the definition above.

Example 1.2.3 Clifford algebra over a vector space
#

Let \(V\) be a vector space over \(\mathbb R\), equipped with a quadratic form \(Q\).

Since \(\mathbb R\) is a commutative ring and \(V\) is a module, definition 1.2.1 of Clifford algebra applies.
